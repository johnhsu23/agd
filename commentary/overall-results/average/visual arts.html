Average Responding Score Results
Average visual arts responding score for eighth-graders not significantly different compared to 2008

Visual arts knowledge and skills include understanding the meaning of visual form and how it is conveyed, as well as understanding how to construct or interpret meaning in visual form. The visual arts portion of the 2016 arts assessment measured students’ ability to respond to art as well as their ability to create original artwork. For example, a responding question asked students to analyze the aesthetic properties of two self-portraits created by twentieth-century artists and to identify technical similarities between the two artworks. In an example creating question, students were asked to create their own self-portrait.

Read more about the responding and creating processes in the 2016 arts assessment.

The average visual arts responding score for eighth-grade students in 2016 was 149, which was not significantly different from 2008, the previous assessment year. The NAEP visual arts scale ranges from 0–300. The NAEP visual arts scale is based on responding questions only.

Because the music and visual arts are distinct disciplines, the responding scales for each were developed independently and the results therefore cannot be compared. Read more about how the NAEP scales in music and visual arts are set.
