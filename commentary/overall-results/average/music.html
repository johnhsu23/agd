Average music responding score for eighth-graders not significantly different compared to 2008

The average music responding scale score for eighth-grade students in 2016 was 147, which was not significantly different from 2008, the previous assessment year. The NAEP music scale ranges from 0â€“300.

Learn more about the knowledge and skills demonstrated by students performing at different scale scores on the music portion of the assessment.

Music responding questions in the 2016 arts assessment asked students to evaluate musical performances, identify and explain musical notation, describe the emotion or mood of a composition, and identify the region of origin or social context of a musical excerpt. For example, after listening to a piece of music, students were asked to identify the music's region of origin and to describe a feature of the music that helped them to identify the part of the world that they selected.

Read more about the music portion of the 2016 arts assessment. Trend results for multiple-choice questions from the 1997, 2008, and 2016 arts assessments are available in the data tables at the bottom of this report.

NOTE:

  • The arts assessment included one music creating question; therefore, only average scale scores for the responding process were reported for the 2016 arts assessment. Results for the creating music questions as well as other questions from the 2016 arts assessment are available in the NAEP Questions Tool.   • The responding scales for music and visual arts were developed independently because music and visual arts are distinct disciplines.
