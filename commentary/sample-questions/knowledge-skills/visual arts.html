Understanding the knowledge and skills behind the visual arts responding scores

A more in-depth understanding of what students know and can do in relation to the content and processes in responding to visual arts may be gained by viewing the knowledge and skills demonstrated by student performance at different score points on the visual arts responding scale. Scores for the 25th and 75th percentiles are also shown.

The descriptions listed below indicate some of the visual arts knowledge and skills demonstrated by eighth grade students in answering responding questions administered as part of the 2016 arts assessment. For example, eighth-grade students who achieved a score of 297 on the visual arts portion of the 2016 arts assessment were likely to correctly answer a question that required them to "label and describe one or two features of a Bearden collage that the artist wants you to notice." These students would also be likely to correctly answer questions associated with a score lower than 297.

Questions from the 2016 arts assessment are available in the NAEP Questions Tool. See an item map with more descriptions of students' visual arts skills. Read more about the framework that guided the development of the assessment questions.
